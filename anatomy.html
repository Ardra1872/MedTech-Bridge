Skeletal System

Explore the human skeletal system in 3D. Click on any bone to learn more.

  • Function: Support, movement, and protection
  • Bones: 206 in an adult human
  • Joints: Enable movement and flexibility
  • Bone Types: Long, short, flat, irregular
  • Role: Produces blood cells in bone marrow

Muscular System

The muscular system enables movement and maintains posture through muscle contraction.

  • Muscle Types: Skeletal, smooth, and cardiac
  • Muscles: Over 600, working in pairs
  • Tendons: Connect muscles to bones
  • Key Functions: Movement, heat production, stability
  • Fibers: Contract to generate force

Circulatory System

The circulatory system delivers oxygen and nutrients via blood flow.

  • Heart: Pumps blood throughout the body
  • Blood Vessels: Arteries, veins, capillaries
  • Blood: Carries oxygen, nutrients, and waste
  • Key Functions: Transport, temperature regulation
  • Circulation: Systemic and pulmonary loops

Nervous System

The nervous system coordinates body functions through signals.

  • Brain: Controls thought, memory, and emotion
  • Spinal Cord: Transmits nerve signals
  • Nerves: Network of signal carriers
  • Key Functions: Communication, reflexes, sensation
  • Neurons: Basic units of signal transmission
